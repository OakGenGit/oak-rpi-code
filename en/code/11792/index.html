Eco-Friendly Choices

Fact: Recycling one aluminium can saves enough energy to power a TV for 3 hours.

Use reusable bags, bottles, and containers instead of single-use plastics.

Recycling Tips

Plastic recycling helps reduce pollution and conserves resources. Always clean and dry your plastic before recycling to prevent contamination.

Commonly recycled plastics include bottles, containers, and packaging. Check your local council’s recycling guide to see which plastics are accepted.

Avoid recycling soft plastics like plastic bags through curbside bins — these often require special drop-off points.

Paper and cardboard are widely recycled materials. Make sure to flatten boxes and remove any non-paper packaging (e.g., plastic windows from envelopes).

Wet or dirty paper (like used tissues or greasy pizza boxes) should not be recycled as it can spoil the whole batch.

Reuse cardboard boxes for storage or crafts before recycling to reduce waste.

Electronics contain valuable metals and hazardous materials. Never put them in regular bins.

Take old phones, laptops, and batteries to dedicated e-waste recycling centres or participating shops that collect them.

Before recycling, ensure you remove any personal data from devices.
